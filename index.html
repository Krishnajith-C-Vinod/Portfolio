About Me

I am from Kozhikode, Kerala, India. I completed my BSc in Physics from Zamorins Guruvayurappan College and later pursued my MSc in Physics at Government Arts and Science College Meenchanda, where I graduated as the First Rank Holder under the University of Calicut in 2025. I am currently pursuing my PhD in Physics at Indian Institute of Technology Madras.

My research interests revolve around quantum information science, intelligent systems, and emergent computation. I work on adaptive quantum teleportation protocols, quantum machine learning, quantum neural networks, and unconventional computational structures inspired by quantum mechanics. I am particularly interested in exploring how quantum systems can be utilized for intelligent information processing under realistic noisy environments and how new computational paradigms may emerge beyond conventional approaches.

Driven by curiosity and foundational inquiry, I aim to explore the deeper connections between quantum mechanics, computation, and intelligence while studying how quantum systems can transform information processing and intelligent behavior. Alongside research, I also enjoy scientific outreach, explaining complex physics concepts in intuitive ways, and working on educational demonstrations related to quantum mechanics.

Benchmarking Quantum Teleportation Schemes : A Noise-Based Comparative Approach (MSc. Project)

This project investigates the impact of environmental noise on quantum teleportation by analyzing the average fidelity of different quantum states under Bit Flip, Amplitude Damping, Phase Flip, and Depolarizing noise models using a custom Python library, QPy, to simulate teleportation protocols and evaluate the robustness of quantum communication systems in noisy environments.
